## 2. **EU BLUE STRIPE ON BOTH SIDES** (Left and Right Stripes)
Plates with distinctive blue stripes on both the left AND right edges.

- **Italy**: Italy's license plates are recognizable by the light blue vertical stripes on both the left and right edges
- **Albania**: Albania's license plates are recognizable by the light blue vertical stripes on both the left and right edges
- **France**: (lighter stripe on right side compared to left)

---

## 3. **EU BLUE LEFT + YELLOW RIGHT STRIPE**
Special combination of blue stripe on left and yellow stripe on right.

- **Portugal**: Portugal is the only country with yellow on the right (in addition to blue on the left)

---

## 4. **RED STRIPE ON LEFT SIDE**
Plates featuring red stripes, typically on the left side.

- **Isle of Man**: The Isle of Man has yellow rear plates with a red stripe on the left
- **Israel**: Israel has a blue vertical stripe on the left of their yellow plates (also yellow overall)
- **Kyrgyzstan**: Kyrgyzstan has a red stripe on the left
- **Ukraine** (partial): Blue on top, yellow on bottom in the stripe area
- **Palestine**: Elongated white plates

---

## 5. **RED PLATES OVERALL**
- **Belgium**: Belgium has the standard European plates with a unique red lettering (appears as a faint red blur on Street View)
- **Brazil** (commercial vehicles): In Brazil, commercial vehicles use distinctive red license plates
- **Bhutan**: Plates from Bhutan have a unique red colour

---

## 6. **YELLOW PLATES** (Full or Dominant)

### Yellow with Blue Stripe:
- **Luxembourg**: Luxembourg uses yellow license plates (front and back) with the EU strip on the left side
- **Israel**: Israel has yellow license plates, both front and back. They have a blue vertical stripe on the left of their yellow plates
- **Qatar**: (maroon band on left)

### Yellow Plates (Various):
- **Laos**: Laos typically has yellow license plates
- **Japan**: Japan often has yellow plates
- **Colombia**: Colombia uses yellow license plates
- **Christmas Island**: Both front and rear license plates on Christmas Island are yellow colour
- **Ghana** (commercial): Commercial vehicles in Ghana have yellow license plates and are pretty common
- **Sri Lanka** (rear): Rear license plates in Sri Lanka are yellow. Front license plates are white
- **Kenya** (rear, square): Kenya has yellow, square rear license plates. Front license plates are long & white
- **Uganda** (rear): License plates in Uganda are white in the front and yellow in the back
- **Hong Kong** (rear): Hong Kong license plates are almost square shaped. They have yellow rear plates and white front plates

---

## 7. **LONG WHITE PLATES** (Standard elongated white)
Standard elongated white plates without special EU stripes or colors.

- **Iceland**: Iceland stands out as one of the few European countries where license plates lack the blue vertical strip on the left side. Icelandic license plates are elongated and white
- **Russia**: All-white plates with no EU stripe
- **Switzerland**: Switzerland is one of the rare European countries that lack the blue vertical stripe of Europe on their license plates. Front Swiss license plates are typically less elongated than the rest of Europe
- **Pakistan**: (trekker coverage - person held camera)
- **Palestine**: Palestine has elongated white plates
- **Brazil** (regular vehicles): Standard license plates in Brazil are either entirely white (pre-2018) or white with a blue strip on top
- **Indonesia**
- **Philippines** (mixed with black sections)
- **Chile**
- **Argentina**: Non-commercial vehicles have long white plates, sometimes with a top blue bar. Some plates in Argentina have a black blob in the middle

---

## 8. **SHORT WHITE PLATES** (Less elongated)
Plates that are more compact or square-shaped.

- **Andorra**: Andorran license plates are less elongated than typical European plates. They also lack the blue vertical stripe of standard European plates. The left side of Andorran plates feature an orange blotch
- **Malta**: Cars in Malta have a mixture of standard European plates and plates that are less elongated with a blue section in the upper-left corner
- **Hong Kong**: Hong Kong license plates are almost square shaped

---

## 9. **BLACK PLATES**
- **Malaysia**: In Malaysia, license plates typically have a black background with two blocks of white text
- **Tunisia**: Tunisia has black plates that are narrow and long
- **Qatar**: (mostly long and elongated with maroon band on left)

---

## 10. **MIXED/SPECIAL COLORING**

### Blue and White Mixed:
- **Brazil** (newer vehicles): Standard license plates in Brazil are either entirely white (pre-2018) or white with a blue strip on top
- **Argentina**: Argentina has two very useful plate types to remember: one with what looks like a blurry black dot in the centre, and another which has a blue top half

### Multi-colored/Striped:
- **Senegal**: Senegal uses license plates with blue background
- **Rwanda**, **Uganda**, **Kenya**, **Ghana**: Regional variations

### Green Plates:
- **India** (electric vehicles): In India, Electric vehicles have green license plates and transport and trucks have yellow license plates

---

## 11. **UNIQUE/DISTINCTIVE PATTERNS**

### Color Combinations by Country:
- **Ecuador**: Orange plates (commercial/taxis)
- **Peru**: Similar to other Latin American countries
- **Thailand**: Mixed white plates
- **Myanmar**: Not commonly seen
- **Vietnam**: White plates with various text styles
- **Cambodia**: Cambodia has full coverage

## Notes for GeoGuessr Players

1. Most European countries will have white license plates with a blue badge on the left side
2. The Netherlands, Luxembourg, the UK, Cyprus and Ireland are the only European countries with primarily yellow plates (though only the back license plates in the UK/Cyprus will be yellow)
3. Shape and elongation can be as distinctive as color
4. Front vs. rear plates sometimes differ (especially in Asian countries)
5. Commercial vehicles often have different colored plates than regular vehicles
6. Blue plates often indicate Brazil's newer registration system (post-2018)
